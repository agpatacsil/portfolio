UCSD Recreation

Redesign based on interviews and human centered design.

Overview

For our project, we took a look at the UCSD Recreation website and observed how people navigated through the site pages to sign up for a "Moonlight Kayak" trip. During our interviews, we saw our interviewees primarily making mistakes as opposed to slips, which signified to us that this website in its current form (as of Winter 2018) is not the best designed. In our redesign, we attempt to minimize these errors based on our findings.

Interviews

Location: Geisel Library because we wanted to see how different types of students interact with the website. At the library we believed we would find a range of students, starting from those who rarely take a UCSD recreation class to those that frequently take them. This is in opposition to interviewing at RIMAC gym where the majority are more likely to frequent the UCSD Recreation website.

Task: Add a "Moonlight Kayak" trip to the shopping cart.

Recording Method: we collected the interviewees interaction with the website by recording our laptop screens, while having someone note slips and mistakes in a notebook as well. This made it easier to provide evidence of the slips and mistakes that students made.

Post Interview Questions

When was the last time you used the UCSD Recreation website?

We asked this questions because we knew people who frequent the website are less likely to make mistakes and more likely to make slips because they have more knowledge of the page navigation. It's also important to consider their last time on the webiste (if applicable) because it gives us insight into what people typically look for on the website and previous experience helps us better understand people's feelings toward the website.

How would you describe your experience and why?

We asked this question to gain a clear understanding as to how the interviewee felt while navigating through the site because what we observed might be different from the emotions they felt and thoughts that came to their mind.

Is there anything you would improve/change about this website?

We used this question to further understand the errors that the interviewee went through by prompting them with how they would change it. We believed that by asking for suggestions, the interviewees would likely explain their frustrations with errors in greater detail to help them explain how they would improve the website.

The Data

Additional Notes
  • Shortest completion of task was 1 min 24 sec (interviewee #5)
  • Longest completion of task was 8 min 59 sec (interviewee #6)
  • Most of our interviewees had never been on the website before
  • Most of those who finished quickly had been on the website before. They noted that it was confusing the first time, but once they learned how to correctly navigate it was easy
  • Wanted more descriptions of the activities
  • Liked the pictures that went with the activities and wanted more
