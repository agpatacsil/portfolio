DIABETES DESIGN INITIATIVE

—Empowering Dexcom users to manage diabetes through design




This past fall, I was a research assistant in the UCSD Design Lab as part of their Diabetes Design Initiative team. We partnered with medical device manufacturer Dexcom for a 7-week design sprint to address issues with the company’s continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) system’s mobile interface. Throughout this project, I worked on a smaller team with four other student designers. We conducted user research to identify the most pressing issues that Dexcom’s users were facing. The insights we found led us to focus on designing a solution that empowered users to efficiently manage their diabetes.



This was a very iterative and collaborative design process. Each week, we were able to discuss our ideas with Dexcom’s users and company representatives to make sure we were tackling relevant problems with feasible solutions. At one point, my group actually had to completely change the direction of our project. Our design was innovative and received positive feedback from users, but due to circumstances regarding policies, we couldn’t move forward with it. This put our group behind schedule, but we chose not to view it as a setback! We just viewed it as another step in the design process and a way to improve our versatility as designers.
